1. What is Light?

Light is a form of energy that allows us to see objects.

Luminous objects: Sun, stars
Non‑luminous objects: Moon, book

2. How Light Travels

Light travels in straight lines. This is called rectilinear propagation of light.

Example: Formation of shadows.

3. Reflection of Light

Reflection is the bouncing back of light from a surface.
Law of Reflection:
Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection

4. Types of Mirrors

Plane Mirror: Flat surface mirror.
Image is virtual, upright and same size.
Concave Mirror: Curves inward. Used in shaving mirrors.
Convex Mirror: Curves outward. Used in rear view mirrors.
Quiz
Which mirror is used in cars?
Convex
Concave

5. Important Formulas

Mirror Formula:
1/f = 1/v + 1/u
Magnification:
m = v / u

6. Numerical Example

Object distance = 20 cm
Focal length = 10 cm

Using mirror formula:

1/f = 1/v + 1/u
1/10 = 1/v + 1/20
1/v = 1/20
v = 20 cm

7. Real Life Applications
